Redevelopment of Water and Sewage CRM System

Role: Technical Leader and Senior Developer

Overview: One of the most complex projects I led involved the complete redevelopment of a Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system for a water and sewage company. The existing system, serving over 3 million customers across six provinces, was plagued with performance issues and lacked critical features.

Problem Identification and Solution Consideration: The most pressing issue was in one province with 700,000 customers, where monthly bill calculations took 2.5 seconds each, creating significant bottlenecks. The database locked during these calculations, making the admin, user, and reporting panels unresponsive. Additionally, the reporting system was slow, further degrading user experience.

Planning and Requirement Gathering: To tackle these challenges, the company decided to rewrite the system from scratch, appointing me as the technical lead. Over four months, I collaborated with two expert designers and engaged with domain experts to gather comprehensive business requirements. Our goal was to design a customizable system for different provinces.

Architectural Vision and Detailed Design: After consolidating the requirements, I worked with a senior team member to develop a high-level architectural vision to guide the design and development phases. Key tasks included:

- Defining System Boundaries and Main Components: Establishing clear delineations for each module.

- Identifying Major Technologies and Frameworks: Choosing robust and scalable technologies.

- Outlining Data Flow and Integration Points: Ensuring seamless interaction between different system components.

We documented these in an architectural vision document, which served as our blueprint.

Technology Selection: Before implementation, we selected technologies based on our needs and team expertise:

- MS SQL Server: For its reliability, performance, and advanced enterprise features.

- .NET Framework with C#: For its robust language features, development tools, and potential for cross-platform capabilities.

- Angular: For creating rich, dynamic, and responsive user interfaces.

Implementation and Delivery: We designed a three-layer backend structure, including Web API Restful services with custom error handling, log management, and authentication features. The project followed the Scrum methodology, allowing for iterative development and continuous feedback. An eight-member team diligently worked on this project over two years.

Measuring Success: The success of the new system was evident through several key metrics and outcomes:

- Performance Improvement: Reduced monthly bill calculation time to 1 second per 100 bills, without affecting other system components.

- Customization: Designed for easy customization to meet the varying needs of different provinces.

- Enhanced Reporting: Enabled users to generate reports quickly and as needed, significantly improving operational efficiency.

Conclusion: The new CRM system resolved the previous performance issues and provided a scalable, customizable solution for the future. The project's success was measured through improved calculation speeds, enhanced user experience, and the system's adaptability to different customer needs. This complex project underscored the importance of meticulous planning, robust design, and iterative development in delivering a high-quality software solution.
